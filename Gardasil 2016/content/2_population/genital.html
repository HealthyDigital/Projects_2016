POPULATION LEVEL IMPACT: GENITAL WARTS

Following the introduction of free HPV vaccine for females aged 12-26 years
via the National HPV Vaccination Program, population level impacts are
already being observed.1,2

Proportion of Australian females diagnosed with genital warts at first visit, by age group, 2004-2011

Retrospective analysis of standardised demographic, genital warts
frequency and sexual behaviour information collected at eight public
specialist sexual health services from across Australia. Between 2004
and 2011, 85,770 Australian born patients were seen for the first time
at the 8 sexual health services. Of these 7,686 (9%) patients (2,394
women and 5,292 men) were diagnosed as having genital warts.2

  • Surveillance to 2011 shows a 92.6%
    decline in genital warts diagnoses in
    females less than 21 years of age
    compared to the pre-vaccination period.2
  • A significant decline was also observed
    in females aged 21-30 years.2
