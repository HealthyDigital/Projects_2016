While the most effective time to vaccinate with GARDASIL is before exposure to HPV, there is clinical evidence of benefit in vaccinating those already sexually active1,2,3

  • Subjects already infected with one or more vaccine-related HPV types prior to vaccination were protected from clinical disease caused by the remaining vaccine HPV types1
    •
  • Subjects with evidence of prior infection that had resolved by vaccination onset were protected from reacquisition or recurrence of infection leading to clinical disease1

A retrospective post-hoc analysis showed that women vaccinated with GARDASIL who were diagnosed and treated for HPV related disease (cervical, vulvar and vaginal intraepithelial neoplasia and genital warts) were less likely to develop subsequent HPV related disease.4

  • Reduction in HPV related disease
    irrespective of causal HPV type     35.2% (95% CI 13.8-51.8) - 46.2% (95% CI 22.5-63.2)
  • Reduction in HPV related disease
    related to HPV 6, 11, 16 or 18     64.4% (95% CI 41.6-79.3) - 79.1% (95% CI 49.4-92.8)

Note: GARDASIL is not a therapeutic treatment for HPV disease
