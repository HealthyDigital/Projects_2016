Several economic analyses have show Symbicort® to be cost-effective
or the dominant (more effective at lower cost) treatment for COPD1-4

  • From a societal perspective, Symbicort + Spiriva® was dominant in Denmark, Norway and Sweden; the ICER was estimate at €174 per exacerbation avoided in Finland
  • From a health care perspective, Symbicort + Spiriva® was dominant in Norway; the ICERs were estimated at DKK 1,580 (€212), €307, SEK 1,573 (€165) per exacerbation avoided in Denmark, Finland and Sweden, respectively (2010)
Symbicort Seretide® PATHOS*
(Hedegaard
et al.3)
  • Annual exacerbation rates: 0.800 with Symbicort; 1.090 with Seretide ® (26.6% reduction, P< 0.0001)
  • Symbicort treatment was associated with being more cost-saving compared with Seretide® treatment (total average annual per patient cost of SEK 12,580 [€1,318] and SEK 15,979 [€1,675], respectively)
  • Symbicort was associated with being the dominant strategy (more effective at lower cost)
  • [2010 Swedish unit costs; 2,734 patients in each treatment group]

  • From a health care perspective, the treatment of a patient for one year with Symbicort was associated with a saying of €499.90 (€195.10 for drugs, €193.10 for COPD hospitalisations and €111.70 for pneumonia hospitalisations) corresponding to a 27.6% decrease vs Seretide®
