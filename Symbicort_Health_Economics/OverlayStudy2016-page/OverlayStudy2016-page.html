Welte et al. study design:

  • A 12-week, randomised, double-blind, parallel-group
    study in 660 patients (≥40 yrs) with moderate-severe
    COPD (FEV1 ≤50% predicted normal and a history
    of exacerbations requiring systemic steroids
    and/or antibiotics).
  • Patients received tiotrpium (18Ug) once-daily plus either
    Symbicort® 400/12ug or placebo twice daily. Terbutaline
    0.5mg was used as reliever medication as-needed
    throughout the run in and treatment period. The primary
    outcome was clinic pre-dose FEV1 evaluated as
    change from randomisation to 12 weeks.
  • Over 12 weeks of treatment:1
  • Symbicort 400/12 bd + tiotropium significantly
    prolonged time to first exacerbation vs. tiotropium
    + placebo; p< 0.001
  • Symbicort 400/12 bd + tiotropium significantly
    improved post-dose FEV1 by 11% (at both 5 and
    60 minutes post-dose) vs. tiotropium + placebo;
    p< 0.001
  • Reference: 1. Welte T et al. Am J Respir Crit Care Med. 2009; 180:741-50.
