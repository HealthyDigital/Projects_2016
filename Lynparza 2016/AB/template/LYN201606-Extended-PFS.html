    FOR ORAL MAINTENANCE TREATMENT ADMINISTRATION4

    • More patients in the olaparib group than in the placebo
      group had dose interruptions
      (49 [36%] of 136 vs 21 [16%]
      of 128) or dose reductions 57
      [42%] vs 28 [22%]): vomiting,
      nausea, and fatigue were the
      most common causes of dose
      interruptions or reductions in the
      olaparib group.3,4

      Serious adverse events were reported in
      25 (18%) of 136 patients in the olaparib
      group and 11 (9%) of 128 patients in
      the placebo group; the most common
      serious adverse event was small intestinal
      obstruction (two [1%] patients in the
      olaparib group and three [2%] in the
      placebo group.

      monotherapy for patients

      were generally mild to moderate
