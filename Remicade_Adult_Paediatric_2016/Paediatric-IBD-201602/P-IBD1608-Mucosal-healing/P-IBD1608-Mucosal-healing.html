Study design

Hyams, 2012: Patients (6–17 years old) who had active ulcerative colitis (Mayo scores of 6–12; endoscopic subscores ≥2) and had not responded to or tolerated conventional treatment were given 5 mg/kg REMICADE at weeks 0, 2, and 6. At week 8, only responders were randomly assigned to groups given REMICADE q8w or q12w) and followed through week 54. The primary end point was response at week 8 (decreases in Mayo scores ≥30% and ≥3 points and decreases in rectal bleeding subscores of ≥1 or an absolute subscore of ≤1). Secondary endpoints included Mayo clinical remission (defined as a score of ≤2 with no individual subscore >1), mucosal healing (defined as Mayo endoscopy subscore of 0 or 1), PUCAI clinical remission (defined as a score<10) and the evaluation of clinical remission at week 54. Maintenance end points included Paediatric UC activity index scores <10 points, defined as remission.1
