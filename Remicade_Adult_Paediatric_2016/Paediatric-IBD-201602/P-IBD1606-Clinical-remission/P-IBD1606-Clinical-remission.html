Study design

Hyams, 2012: Patients (6–17 years old) who had active ulcerative colitis (Mayo scores of 6–12; endoscopic subscores ≥2) and had not responded to or tolerated conventional treatment were given 5 mg/kg REMICADE at weeks 0, 2, and 6. The primary end point was response at week 8 (decreases in Mayo scores ≥30% and ≥3 points and decreases in rectal bleeding subscores of ≥1 or an absolute subscore of ≤1). At week 8, only responders were randomly assigned to groups given REMICADE q8w or q12w) and followed through week 54. Patients who lost response during the maintenance phase were eligible to increase their REMICADE dose and/or frequency. Patients receiving REMICADE 5 mg/kg q8w increased to 10 mg/kg q8w. Patients receiving REMICADE 5 mg/kg q12w who lost response within 8 weeks of the previous REMICADE infusion had the dose and frequency increased to 10 mg/kg REMICADE q8w, whereas patients in the same group who lost response between 8 and 12 weeks after the previous infusion continued to receive 5 mg/kg REMICADE but increased the frequency of infusions to q8w. Maintenance end points included PUCAI scores <10 points, defined as remission.1
