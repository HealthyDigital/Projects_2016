REMICADE continuous maintenance therapy may improve
colectomy-free survival1

The probability of being colectomy-free with continuous maintenance REMICADE is reported to be
88% (at 3 months), 88% (at 6 months), 84% (at 12 months) and 74% (at 24 months).1

Study design

Hyams, 2010: A prospective, multicentre, inception cohort study of a subgroup of children with ulcerative colitis enrolled in the Paediatric Inflammatory Bowel Disease Collaborative Research Group Registry. This study included 332 children diagnosed with ulcerative colitis enrolled in the registry between January 2002 and August 2008. In this study, data collected at diagnosis from Registry patients with ulcerative colitis include age; sex; disease activity as measured by physician global assessment; and use of REMICADE, corticosteroids, immunomodulators (azathioprine/6- mercaptopurine), and 5-aminosalicylates.1
