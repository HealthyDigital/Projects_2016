Study design

Armuzzi, 2013: 12 month, open-label trial of 126 patients (90 consecutive patients prospectively enrolled and 36 retrospectively included) with moderate-severe (by endoscopy) steroid-dependent active ulcerative colitis treated with either: monotherapy with REMICADE 5 mg/kg at weeks 0, 2 and 6, then 8 weekly; or combination therapy with REMICADE at same schedule plus AZA 2-2.5 mg/kg/d. Co-primary endpoints were rates of 1) steroid-free clinical remission at 6 and 12 months, 2) steroid-free clinical remission and mucosal healing at 12 months, and 3) colectomy within 12 months. Subgroup analysis was performed separately for thiopurine-naive vs thiopurine-exposed and combination vs REMICADE therapy. Definitions: Steroid dependence – the inability to reduce steroid dose below equivalent of prednisolone 10 mg/d within 3 months of starting steroids without recurrent active disease or relapse within 3 months; Steroid-free clinical remission – the absence of active disease without steroids for the past 3 months.1
