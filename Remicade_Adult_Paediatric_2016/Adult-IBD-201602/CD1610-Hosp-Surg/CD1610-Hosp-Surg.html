Surgery: The inevitable outcome for most patients
with Crohn’s disease1

  • 75%1 Proportion of patients reported to require an intestinal resection
    for complications related to stricturing or penetrating disease.1
  • 73-85%2 Proportion of patients reported to have endoscopic evidence of
    recurrent Crohn’s disease 1-3 years after intestinal resection.2
    Histological recurrence may occur as early as 1 week after surgery.3
  • 60%1 Proportion of patients in which clinical recurrence is reported
    10 years after surgery. At 3 years, this proportion is 33%.1
  • 70%1 Proportion of patients reported to suffer recurrences
    severe enough to warrant repeat surgery by 20 years.1
