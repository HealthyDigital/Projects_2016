Preference

Efficacy and dosing frequency rank highly in
CD patients’ priorities for biologic treatment1

A prospective internet-based survey of 252 adult participants to quantify biologic preferences and
priorities of patients with moderate to severe Crohn’s disease1

The three attributes of greatest importance in accounting for patients’ biologic therapy choice were:1
  • Greater proportion of patients achieving lasting remission
  • Decreased frequency of medication administration
  • Higher proportion of patients responding in 2 weeks

Study design

Prospective internet-based survey of 252 adult participants with moderate to severe Crohn’s disease, 202 of whom were biologic naïve. In a discrete-choice-based conjoint exercise, respondents were asked to select a preferred biologic treatment option from each of 16 pairs of drug profiles that systematically varied efficacy, convenience, and product history attributes. Respondents were also asked to directly rank each specific attribute.
