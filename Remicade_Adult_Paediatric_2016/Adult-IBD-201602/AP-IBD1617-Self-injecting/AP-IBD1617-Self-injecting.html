Preference

Fear of self-injecting ranks highly in IBD patients’ who preferred REMICADE1

Patients who stated a preference for REMICADE most commonly
cited the following reasons:1

  • 67% “I do not like the idea of self-injecting”
  • 42% “I prefer to take the drug less often”
  • 36% “I prefer the convenience of this choice”
In this prospective survey, 42% of patients indicated a preference for REMICADE while 24% of patients indicated a preference for
adalimumab, but this difference was not significant (p=0.07). A third
of patients did not indicate any preference for either anti-TNF therapy1
Adapted from Allen, 2010.1

Study design

Prospective survey of 125 patients with inflammatory bowel disease undertaken over a period of 12 months, with 78 responses included in the analysis. 10 patients had previously been on a biologic – all REMICADE. Patients were posed a hypothetical situation and asked to choose between anti- TNF drugs based on intravenous or subcutaneous administration and dose frequency. The possible outcomes were as follows: (a) infliximab [iv] in hospital every 8 weeks approximately, (b) adalimumab [sc] every 2 weeks at home, or (c) no preference for biological therapy.
