• 1993

    First Australian patients enrolled in Arimidex clinical trials for
    advanced breast cancer in
    postmenopausal women1

  • 1996

    Arimidex approved in Australia as treatment of advanced breast
    cancer in postmenopausal women
    with disease progression following
    tamoxifen therapy.1

  • 1997

    Arimidex gains PBS listing for the treatment of advanced breast
    cancer in postmenopausal women
    with disease progression following
    tamoxifen therapy.2

  • 2000

    Arimidex approved in Australia as 1st line therapy for advanced breast
    cancer in postmenopausal women1

  • 2002

    Arimidex receives PBS listingas first line treatment of hormone-dependent
    advanced breast cancer in postmenopausal women2

    ATAC (Arimidex, Tamoxifen Alone or in
    Combination) Trial     - first results published3

  • 2003

    Arimidex approved in Australia as adjuvant therapy for early breast
    cancer in postmenopausal women1,3

  • 2004

    Arimidex receives PBS listingto include the treatment of
    hormone-dependent early breast
    cancer in postmenopausal women
    in whom tamoxifen therapy is
    contraindicated or in patients
    whom tamoxifen is not tolerated.2

  • 2005

    Arimidex PBS listing extended to include all postmenopausal women
    with hormone-dependent early breast
    cancer based on its cost-effectiveness
    compared to tamoxifen2

  • 2006

    Publication of ATAC endometrial subprotocol results afer 2 years
    of treatment4*

    * the ATAC endometrial subprotocol was initiated
    to establish the background prevalence of
    pathology, and to assess prospectively the
    incidence and nature of intrauterine changes
    before and following endocrine therapy.

  • 2008

    Publication of 100 months of follow-up from the ATAC
    trial: long-term outcome5

  • 2010

    Publication of 120 months of follow-up of the ATAC
    trial: long-term outcomes6
