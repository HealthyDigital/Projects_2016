Zoladex® 3.6mg in Early Premenopausal
Breast Cancer1-5#sup>

#Pre and perimenopausal

  • As effective as CMF* (chemotherapy) for disease-free survival (DFS) in ER+ early breast cancer2,3
  • HR=1.05; 95% CI:0.88–1.24; P=0.5973
  • Improved overall quality of life (QoL) compared to CMF# during the first six months of therapy4†
  • †QoL was evaluated using a written, self-administered questionnaire (Rotterdam Symptom Checklist (RSCL))4
  • Improved tolerability compared with CMF*2‡
  • ‡Zoladex had a lower incidence of side effects usually associated with chemotherapy, including nausea/ vomiting, alopecia and infections, during CMF treatment period. Zoladex causes a higher incidence of side effects related to oestrogen suppression, such as hot flushes, vaginal dryness and soreness, but these incidences decrease below those seen in CMF patients within 6 months of completing therapy.2
  • The majority of patients have a return to menses within a year of completing Zoladex therapy2

*cyclophosphamide, methotrexate and fluorouracil
